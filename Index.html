Manali Trip-Himachal Pradesh

Once upon a time, Manali was a backpacking capital of India. A dirt track from Manali to Kullu was part of the popular hippie trail. Today it is tough to imagine Manali as a backpacker’s paradise. It has easily become one of the most popular hill stations in India with tourists thronging to Manali in the height of summer as well as winter.

One of the most popular tourist places in Northern India is situated in Kullu District, Himachal Pradesh on the banks of Beas River and at an altitude of 1950 Meters or 6500 Feet (approximately) Manali, Valley of Gods. The term stands for Manu (Manu Rishi) + Aali (Home) and I bet you his home (Manali ;)) is just MAGICAL in nature.Manali suits the needs of every kind of travel mindset. A family looking for some bonding time, a couple for some peace and quiet, solo travellers for some solitude or a group of friends seeking an adventure.

  1. River Rafting on the Beas River

    River rafting in Manali is one of the top things to do in Manali. The Beas River flowing through the mountain’s terrain is full of thrilling adventures. Gear up for the thrill of surviving the swift waters on a raft. This experience is among the favorite memories of many travelers on holiday in Manali. Also, River Beas is among the best stretches for rafting in India. Furthermore, there are short distances for beginner’s level as well as the challenging ones for people pro at adventure. Also, Pirdi has a dedicated water sports center that provides a wide range of adventure activities.

    Best time for river rafting in Manali:

    The best river rafting in Manali happens to be in June and July. While monsoon does restrict visitors for a couple of months, it restarts by late October and goes on till early February.
